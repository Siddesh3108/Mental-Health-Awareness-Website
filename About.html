Depression

Depression is a common mental disorder that causes people to experience depressed mood, loss of interest or pleasure, feelings of guilt or low self-worth, disturbed sleep or appetite, low energy, and poor concentration.

Anxiety

Anxiety is a normal and often healthy emotion. However, when a person regularly feels disproportionate levels of anxiety, it might become a medical disorder. Anxiety disorders lead to excessive nervousness, fear, apprehension, and worry.

Sleep Disorders

Sleep disorders encompass various conditions that affect sleep quality and patterns. These may include insomnia, sleep apnea, narcolepsy, and restless leg syndrome. Sleep disorders can lead to fatigue, difficulty concentrating, and impaired daily functioning.

Stress

Stress is a natural response to challenges or demands. However, chronic stress can have detrimental effects on mental and physical health. It can lead to anxiety, depression, headaches, muscle tension, and other health issues if not managed effectively.

Trauma

Trauma refers to a deeply distressing or disturbing experience that overwhelms an individual's ability to cope. Traumatic events may include accidents, natural disasters, abuse, or violence. Trauma can lead to a range of emotional and psychological symptoms, including flashbacks, nightmares, and feelings of fear or helplessness.
